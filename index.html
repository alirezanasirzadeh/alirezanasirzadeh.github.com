About

Hello, and welcome to my digital space! I am Ali, a biomechanics expert deeply passionate about working at the intersection of sports and movement biomechanics and wearable robotics. My research focuses on designing and biomechanically validating exosuits and assistive devices that actively improve human movement, dynamic balance, and athletic performance. To fully understand how the human body moves, I combine advanced biomechanical measurements and detailed musculoskeletal modeling to evaluate gait, dynamic stability, and joint loading. Ultimately, my goal is to translate these complex biomechanical insights into intuitive, human-centered wearable technology.

Research Highlights

Passive Exosuit for Golf Swing Performance

Designed and validated a passive exosuit that harnesses elastic resistance to enhance golf-swing biomechanics and power output.

Journal of Biomechanics, 2025

Hip-Abduction Exosuit for Balance

Co-developed a soft exosuit providing hip-abduction assistance that lowers metabolic cost and improves balance during human walking.

Science Robotics, 2023

Exosuit Assistance for Downhill Walking

Showed that passive exosuit assistance reduces tibiofemoral joint loading and improves dynamic stability during demanding downhill walking.

J. NeuroEngineering & Rehabilitation, 2026

H-Medi Mediolateral Balance Exosuit

Rapid hip-abduction torque to recover mediolateral standing and walking balance without stepping — for fall prevention.

Under review · Science Advances

Real-Time User Adaptation (IMU + LSTM)

Estimating user adaptation during hip-exosuit-assisted walking from wearable IMU data using long short-term memory deep-learning models.

Biomimetics, 2026

Biomimetic Footwear & Ankle Support

Custom high-collar shoe and active variable-compression footwear improving muscle activity, leg stiffness, dynamic stability, and ankle-sprain prevention.

Biomimetics · PLOS ONE
